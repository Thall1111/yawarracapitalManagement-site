Sovereign Capital for a Nature-Positive Future.

51% Indigenous-owned, AFSL-compliant. Delivering Dual Alpha - Cultural & Financial.

Mission

To accelerate Australia’s transition to a nature-positive economy by pairing sovereign Indigenous governance with institutional fiduciary discipline. We regenerate land, restore water, decarbonise industry, and create auditable shared value for communities and investors.

Vision

Enable Indigenous sovereignty as foundation for Australia’s next economy by embedding cultural authority into climate finance, infrastructure investment and Indigenous enterprise. Majority Indigenous-owned through the Cultural Custodian Council (CCC) and formally approved by Wiradjuri Elders, YCM partners with Frontier Impact Group (FIG, AFSL trustee) to deliver Australia’s first Indigenous-majority, AFSL-aligned fund platform.

Differentiators

  • Dual Rail Governance: Sovereign governance (CCC, ORIC) + fiduciary governance (ASIC, AFSL).
  • Sovereign Certification: CAF certification eliminates black-cladding risk.
  • Scalable Platform: From pooled funds (YCM, YARNT) to project-level SPVs.
  • Traceability & Audit: Approvals, decisions and certifications logged with integrity controls.

Projects

Flagship initiatives across funds and SPVs in energy transition, nature repair and circular systems. All proceed through the Cultural Assurance Framework (CAF) and enter investment-ready.

Yawarra Capital Management (YCM)

Indigenous-led manager (Hybrid ORIC–ASIC, 51% Indigenous ownership) focused on clean energy, nature repair and low-carbon infrastructure.

  • Mandate: Energy transition, nature repair, low-carbon materials.
  • Governance: Dual-rail (ORIC + ASIC/AFSL); Indigenous ownership and board oversight.
  • Pipeline: Multi-asset SPVs and programmatic capital with institutional partners.
  • Reporting: Quarterly investor reporting aligned to CAF outcomes and KPIs.

YARNT Fund

Joint initiative with institutional partners to accelerate a nature-positive economy with Indigenous governance embedded from origination.

  • Focus: Landscape repair, biodiversity, regenerative agriculture, carbon-aligned projects.
  • Instruments: Equity, revenue-share and outcome-based structures suited to project stage.
  • Measurement: Nature and community outcomes mapped to CAF gates and investor KPIs.
  • Partnerships: Delivery partners across MRV, land management and local employment.

Energy & Infrastructure SPVs

Bankable platforms across generation, storage and enabling infrastructure, structured for institutional capital and community benefits.

  • Verticals: Solar, wind, storage, waste-to-energy, microgrids and enabling infrastructure.
  • Contracts: O&M and AM optimisation; grid and offtake strategies aligned to risk allocation.
  • Community: Local jobs, procurement targets and cultural heritage management embedded.
  • Exit: Hold/operate or recycle with pre-agreed rights and reporting continuity.

Nature Repair, Water & Circularity

High-integrity restoration, water systems and circular materials projects with traceable community and ecological outcomes.

  • Nature Repair: Biodiversity corridors, soil regeneration, revegetation and cultural fire practices.
  • Water & Waste: Efficiency, treatment, reuse and circular systems including WtE where appropriate.
  • Materials: Low-carbon materials and logistics with supplier transition programs.
  • MRV: Fit-for-purpose measurement and reporting linked to CAF and investor KPIs.

Project Development & Accelerator

Support for Indigenous and aligned entrepreneurs across two governance rails: YCCC (sovereign) and YCM (fund manager).

Overview

  1. Initial Screening: Mandate fit, counterparties, cultural map.
  2. Consent & Cultural Gate: Custodian engagement, consent documentation, cultural knowledge register.
  3. Risk & Alignment: Cultural, legal, regulatory and financial risks; benefits and equity pathways.
  4. Due Diligence: Technical, financial, legal and governance validation with community review loop.
  5. Investment Structuring: Finalise SPV/fund terms; embed benefit-sharing and reporting.

Project Screening (lite)

A concise gate to confirm cultural, strategic and fiduciary fit before deeper work.

  • Mandate & thematic fit (energy, nature repair, water/waste, materials)
  • Cultural authority identified; consent pathway and custodians mapped
  • Counterparties & governance clarified
  • Stage & timing: readiness, permits, land access, critical path
  • Capital ask & use defined: amount, structure, deployment timeline
  • Data room readiness (financials, technical, legal, ESG)
  • Early risks logged (cultural, legal, regulatory, commercial)
  • Next step agreed: diligence scope, owners, timeline

Cultural Assurance Framework (CAF)

The Cultural Assurance Framework operationalises FPIC, ICIP and benefit pathways. Projects clear these gateways prior to capital deployment.

  • FPIC: Free, Prior and Informed Consent via culturally legitimate processes; revocable until execution.
  • ICIP & Data Sovereignty: Custodian-governed knowledge, data and symbols embedded in contracting and reporting.
  • Equity & Benefits: Indigenous ownership at manager/SPV level (as appropriate), revenue participation, jobs and procurement targets.
  • Dual Governance: ORIC (sovereign) with ASIC/AFSL (fiduciary) to avoid black-cladding.
  • Traceability: Version-controlled approvals and KPIs, audit-ready artefacts.
