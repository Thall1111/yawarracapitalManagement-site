Overview

  1. Initial Screening: Mandate fit, counterparties, cultural map.
  2. Consent & Cultural Gate: Custodian engagement, consent documentation, cultural knowledge register.
  3. Risk & Alignment: Cultural, legal, regulatory and financial risks; benefits and equity pathways.
  4. Due Diligence: Technical, financial, legal and governance validation with community review loop.
  5. Investment Structuring: Finalise SPV/fund terms; embed benefit-sharing and reporting.

Project Screening (lite)

A concise, plain-English gate to confirm cultural, strategic and fiduciary fit before deeper work.

  • Mandate & thematic fit (energy, nature repair, water/waste, materials)
  • Cultural authority identified; consent pathway and custodians mapped
  • Counterparties & governance clarified
  • Stage & timing: readiness, permits, land access, critical path
  • Capital ask & use defined: amount, structure, deployment timeline
  • Data room readiness checked (financials, technical, legal, ESG)
  • Early risks logged (cultural, legal, regulatory, commercial)
  • Next step agreed: diligence scope, owners, and timeline

Cultural Assurance Framework (CAF)

The Cultural Assurance Framework operationalises FPIC, ICIP and benefit pathways. Projects clear these gateways prior to capital deployment.

  • FPIC: Free, Prior and Informed Consent via culturally legitimate processes; revocable until execution.
  • ICIP & Data Sovereignty: Custodian-governed knowledge, data and symbols reflected in contracting and reporting.
  • Equity & Benefits: Indigenous ownership at manager/SPV level, revenue participation, jobs and procurement targets.
  • Dual Governance: ORIC (sovereign) with ASIC/AFSL (fiduciary) to avoid black-cladding.
  • Traceability: Version-controlled approvals and KPIs, audit-ready artifacts.
